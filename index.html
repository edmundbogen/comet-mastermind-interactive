GETTING STARTED WITH COMET

INSTALLATION & SETUP (15 Minutes)

Step 1: Download Comet Browser

Go to www.perplexity.ai/comet in your current browser

Click "Download for Windows" or "Download for Mac"

Wait for the installer to download (approximately 150MB)

Locate the file in your Downloads folder

Step 2: Install Comet

For Windows:

  • Double-click the downloaded .exe file
  • Click "Yes" when Windows asks for permission
  • Follow the installation wizard (click "Next" → "Install" → "Finish")

For Mac:

  • Double-click the downloaded .dmg file
  • Drag the Comet icon into your Applications folder
  • Right-click Comet in Applications and select "Open" (first time only)
  • Click "Open" when macOS asks for confirmation

Step 3: Initial Setup

Launch Comet from your desktop or Applications folder

Sign in or create a Perplexity account (free)

  • Use your business email for professional use
  • Verify your email address

Import your bookmarks (optional but recommended)

  • Click "Import" when prompted
  • Select your previous browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge, Firefox)
  • Choose what to import (bookmarks, passwords, history)

Step 4: Customize Your Workspace

Pin the AI Sidebar: Click the Perplexity icon on the right side to keep it visible

Set Comet as your default browser (recommended for full benefits)

  • Settings → Make Default Browser

Install your essential Chrome extensions

  • Click the puzzle icon → Visit Chrome Web Store
  • Install your MLS access tools, CRM extensions, etc.

UNDERSTANDING THE INTERFACE

Main Components:

  • Address Bar: Standard web browsing (top)
  • AI Sidebar: Your assistant (right side) - This is where the magic happens
  • Workspaces: Organize tabs by project (top left)
  • Extensions: Same as Chrome (top right)

The AI Sidebar (Your New Best Friend):

  • Always available with one click
  • Sees the page you're currently viewing
  • Can execute commands, research, and generate content
  • Maintains conversation context

USE CASE #1: REAL-TIME PAGE ANALYSIS

Your Live Coaching Assistant

WHY THIS MATTERS

The AI sidebar can analyze ANY page you're viewing and provide instant feedback, catch mistakes, suggest improvements, and coach you in real-time. This turns every moment into a learning and quality-control opportunity.

SCENARIO 1: Reviewing MLS Listings Before Showing

Step-by-Step:

Navigate to your MLS and open a listing you're considering for a client
Click the Perplexity sidebar (or press Ctrl+Shift+P / Cmd+Shift+P)
Type or say: "Analyze this listing for red flags and opportunities"
Review the AI's response for:
  • Pricing concerns
  • Description issues
  • Photo quality problems
  • Days on market insights
  • Neighborhood trends
Advanced Prompts to Try:
• "What questions should I ask the listing agent based on this information?"
• "Compare this listing's description to best practices for luxury properties"
• "What's missing from this listing that buyers in this price range expect?"
• "Summarize the key selling points I should emphasize to my buyer"

Time Saved: 10-15 minutes per listing review

SCENARIO 2: Quality-Checking Your Own Listings

Before You Hit "Publish":

Open your draft listing in the MLS or listing platform
Activate Comet's sidebar
Ask: "Review this listing description and suggest improvements for [target buyer type]"
Specific Prompts:
• "Does this description appeal to first-time buyers? What would make it stronger?"
• "Rewrite this to emphasize investment potential"
• "What emotional triggers am I missing for luxury buyers?"
• "Is this description ADA-compliant and Fair Housing compliant?"
Pro Tip: Copy the improved version, paste it into your listing platform, then ask Comet to "check this revised version for any remaining issues."

SCENARIO 3: Contract Review Assistant

When Reviewing Purchase Agreements:

Open the contract PDF or web form
Ask Comet: "Summarize the key contingencies and deadlines in this contract"
Follow up: "What are the risks to my client in this agreement?"
Use it to prep for client calls: "What should I explain to my buyer/seller about this contract?"
Critical Prompts:
• "Flag any unusual clauses or terms"
• "What questions should I ask the other agent about this offer?"
• "Translate this legal language into plain English for my client"

SCENARIO 4: Learning from Competitor Websites

Competitive Intelligence:

Visit a top-producing agent's website in your market
Ask: "Analyze this agent's value proposition and marketing approach"
Follow-up: "What are they doing well that I should consider?"
Strategic question: "How can I differentiate myself from this approach?"

SCENARIO 5: Email Quality Control

Before Sending Important Emails:

Draft your email in Gmail, Outlook, or your email platform
With the email visible, ask Comet: "Review this email for tone, clarity, and professionalism"
Refine based on feedback
Ask: "Predict potential questions or concerns this email might raise"
Useful Prompts:
• "Make this email more persuasive without being pushy"
• "Is this email appropriate for a luxury client?"
• "Simplify this explanation for a first-time buyer"
• "Add a stronger call-to-action"

SCENARIO 6: Social Media Post Review

Before Publishing:

Open your social media scheduler or platform (Buffer, Hootsuite, Meta Business Suite)
With your draft post visible, ask: "Rate this post for engagement potential and suggest improvements"
Get variations: "Give me 3 alternative versions of this post"

DAILY PRACTICE ROUTINE

Morning Ritual (5 minutes):

  • Open today's featured MLS listings
  • Ask Comet to summarize market changes overnight
  • Review your calendar and ask: "What should I prepare for today's appointments?"

Throughout the Day:

  • Every time you're about to send something important → Ask Comet to review it
  • Before every showing → Ask Comet to analyze the listing
  • After every appointment → Ask Comet to generate follow-up tasks

Evening Review (5 minutes):

  • Open your CRM or task list
  • Ask: "Prioritize tomorrow's follow-ups based on likelihood to close"

TROUBLESHOOTING TIPS

If Comet's analysis seems generic:

  • Be more specific: Instead of "analyze this," try "analyze this for first-time buyer appeal"
  • Give context: "I'm showing this to a family relocating from California with a $800K budget"

If you disagree with the AI:

  • That's okay! Use it as a thinking partner, not gospel
  • Ask: "What would be a counterargument to your suggestion?"

If it misses something obvious:

  • Point it out: "You didn't mention the lack of garage space. How significant is that?"
  • The AI learns from your feedback within the conversation

USE CASE #2: LEAD FOLLOW-UP AUTOMATION

Converting Leads into Clients

WHY THIS MATTERS

80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups, but most agents give up after 2. Comet helps you create personalized, consistent follow-up at scale without sounding robotic.

SETTING UP YOUR FOLLOW-UP SYSTEM

Step 1: Create Your Lead Stages Template

Open Comet sidebar and create this prompt template:

"I need a follow-up email sequence for a [LEAD TYPE] who [SPECIFIC SITUATION]. Tone should be [TONE]. Include 5 touchpoints over 2 weeks."

Example Lead Types:

  • Website inquiry who viewed 3+ properties
  • Open house visitor (qualified buyer)
  • Expired listing owner
  • FSBO considering listing
  • Past client (sphere nurture)

Step 2: Generate Your First Sequence

Sample Prompt:

"I need a follow-up email sequence for a first-time buyer who attended my open house yesterday and loved the property but said they need to 'think about it.' They're pre-approved for $450K. Tone should be helpful and consultative, not pushy. Include 5 touchpoints over 2 weeks."

What You'll Get:

  • Day 1: Thank you + additional property info
  • Day 3: Similar listings + market update
  • Day 7: Check-in + buyer resources
  • Day 10: Urgency (market movement, new listings)
  • Day 14: Soft close (schedule consultation)

Step 3: Personalize the Output

Copy the sequence, then ask:

• "Make email #3 more specific to the South Florida market"
• "Add a personal touch referencing our conversation about schools"
• "Include a case study of another first-time buyer I helped"

LEAD-STAGE-SPECIFIC PROMPTS

For New Inquiries (Within 5 Minutes):

"Write a response to this lead inquiry [paste their message]. Acknowledge their question, provide immediate value, and suggest a brief call. Tone: Professional but warm."

For Post-Showing Follow-Up:

"I just showed [ADDRESS] to [CLIENT NAME]. They liked [WHAT THEY LIKED] but concerned about [CONCERN]. Write a follow-up email addressing their concern with data and suggesting next steps."

For Unresponsive Leads (21+ Days):

"Create a 're-engagement' email for a lead who went cold after our initial conversation. Reference our past interaction about [TOPIC]. Include a compelling reason to reconnect without being desperate."

TIME-SAVING AUTOMATION WORKFLOW

Your New Process:

After every lead interaction, immediately open Comet
Click in the sidebar and say/type: "Draft follow-up for [situation]"
Personalize the top 2-3 sentences
Copy into your email or CRM
Send within 5 minutes of interaction

Pro Tip: Create a "Follow-Up Swipe File"

  • Keep your best Comet-generated emails in a Google Doc
  • Categorize by lead type and stage
  • Build your personal library over 30 days
  • Soon you'll have templates for every situation

USE CASE #3: EXPIRED/FSBO CONVERSION

Mining Gold from Failed Listings

WHY THIS MATTERS

Expired listings and FSBOs are motivated sellers with timing urgency. Comet helps you research their situation and craft compelling, personalized outreach that stands out from generic agent scripts.

THE RESEARCH PHASE

Step 1: Find Your Targets

  • Navigate to your MLS expired listings from the past 30 days
  • Or search Zillow/FSBO sites for "For Sale by Owner" in your market
  • Open the listing page

Step 2: Deep-Dive Analysis with Comet

With the listing visible, ask:

"Analyze why this listing likely expired. Include pricing analysis, marketing assessment, and days on market context for this neighborhood."

Follow-Up Questions:

• "What should the list price have been based on recent comps?"
• "What's missing from the marketing approach?"
• "What objections will the seller have about listing again?"
• "Research the listing agent - what's their track record in this neighborhood?"

CRAFTING YOUR OUTREACH

Step 3: Generate Personalized Contact Letter

Prompt Template:

"Write a personalized letter/email to the owner of [ADDRESS] whose listing just expired after [X] days on market at $[PRICE]. The property is a [DESCRIPTION]. I want to position myself as the solution without criticizing their previous agent. Include:
- Specific data about why it didn't sell
- My unique approach that would be different
- Social proof from similar properties I've sold
- Low-pressure call to action

Tone: Empathetic, consultative, confident."

What Comet Will Generate:

  • Opening that acknowledges their frustration (empathy)
  • 2-3 specific insights about their property/market
  • Your differentiated approach
  • Proof of results in similar situations
  • Soft CTA (free consultation, market analysis)

Step 4: Create Multiple Touchpoints

Once you have your initial outreach, ask:

• "Create a follow-up voicemail script for this same seller"
• "Write a text message version (under 160 characters)"
• "Draft a hand-written note version for a greeting card"
• "Create a video script (60 seconds) for this seller"

FSBO-SPECIFIC APPROACH

Step 1: Research the FSBO Listing

Navigate to their Zillow/FSBO listing and ask:

"Analyze this for-sale-by-owner listing. What mistakes are they making? What will frustrate them most about the FSBO process?"

Step 2: Create Educational Outreach

Prompt:

"Write an educational email to this FSBO seller offering genuine help with [SPECIFIC ISSUE COMET IDENTIFIED]. Position me as a resource, not as someone trying to steal their listing. Include:
- One immediate tip they can implement
- One warning about a common FSBO mistake
- Offer of a free resource (pricing analysis, contract review, etc.)
- No pressure to list with me

Tone: Helpful educator, not salesperson."

THE FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

Week 1: The Educational Approach

  • Email 1: "I noticed your home at [ADDRESS]" + one insight
  • Day 3: Mail a comparative market analysis (CMA)
  • Day 5: Text or voicemail with additional tip

Week 2: The Value Demonstration

  • Email 2: Case study of similar property you sold vs. their approach
  • Day 10: "How it's going?" check-in (genuine curiosity)

Week 3: The Soft Close

  • Email 3: Market update showing time cost of staying on market
  • Final offer: "Free listing presentation - no obligation"
Generate Entire Sequence:
"Create a 3-week follow-up sequence for [EXPIRED or FSBO] at [ADDRESS] using the educational, non-pushy approach we discussed. Include email, voicemail, text, and direct mail components."

OBJECTION HANDLING FOR EXPIRED/FSBO

Common Objections & Comet Solutions:

Objection: "I'm going to wait for the market to improve"

"Create a data-driven response showing the cost of waiting in the current [YOUR MARKET] market. Include interest rate impact, seasonal trends, and opportunity cost. Make it specific to a $[PRICE] property."

Objection: "The last agent didn't do anything"

"Write a response that empathizes with their experience while differentiating my marketing approach. Include specific tactics: [LIST YOUR UNIQUE STRATEGIES]. Avoid criticizing the previous agent."

Objection: "Your commission is too high"

"Create a value-based response to commission objection for a $[PRICE] property. Break down what they get for the commission, include ROI data, and position net proceeds as the important metric, not commission rate."

Objection: "I can sell it myself and save money"

"Write an educational response about the true cost of FSBO, including: time investment, legal liability, pricing accuracy, negotiation mistakes, and average FSBO vs. agent-sold price difference. Use National Association of Realtors data. Tone: Helpful, not condescending."

BUILDING YOUR EXPIRED/FSBO SYSTEM

Daily Routine (15 minutes):

  1. Check MLS for new expireds
  2. Use Comet to analyze top 3 prospects
  3. Generate personalized outreach for each
  4. Send within 24 hours of expiration

Weekly Batch Work (30 minutes):

  1. Review FSBO listings on Zillow/Craigslist
  2. Create educational content pieces (tips, guides)
  3. Build follow-up sequences for all active prospects
  4. Track which messages get responses (optimize over time)

Success Metrics to Track:

  • Response rate to first contact: _____%
  • Appointments set: _____
  • Listings secured: _____
  • Average days from first contact to listing: _____

ADVANCED TIP: THE "INSIDER REPORT"

Create a monthly "Expired Listing Report" using Comet:

"Analyze all expired listings in [NEIGHBORHOOD/ZIP] over the past 90 days. Create a report showing:
- Common reasons for failure
- Average days on market
- Pricing patterns
- Seasonal trends
- Success rate of re-listings
Format as a professional market report I can send to expired listing owners."

Use This Report:

  • As a leave-behind at door knocking
  • As email attachment in outreach
  • As credibility builder in listing presentations
  • Updated quarterly to show expertise

USE CASE #4: COMPETITIVE LISTING ANALYSIS

Winning More Listing Presentations

WHY THIS MATTERS

Sellers choose agents who demonstrate market expertise and strategic thinking. A data-driven competitive analysis positions you as the market authority and wins listings.

PRE-LISTING PRESENTATION PREPARATION

Step 1: Identify the Competition (10 minutes)

Go to your MLS and search for:
  • Active listings in the same neighborhood/subdivision
  • Pending/sold listings from past 90 days
  • Similar bed/bath/sqft configuration within 10% price range
Open 5-10 comparable listings in separate tabs

Step 2: Batch Analysis with Comet

"I'm preparing a listing presentation for [ADDRESS - PROPERTY DESCRIPTION]. I have [NUMBER] comparable listings open in tabs. Analyze these comps and create a competitive positioning report including:
- Price per square foot comparison table
- Days on market trends
- Key features comparison
- Pricing strategy recommendation
- Competitive advantages of my listing
- Potential challenges

Format as a professional report I can present to the seller."

Alternative Approach (Tab-by-Tab):

Navigate to each comp and ask:

• "How does this listing's marketing compare to best practices?"
• "What is this listing doing well that we should consider?"
• "What mistakes is this listing making that we should avoid?"

CREATING YOUR PRICING STRATEGY

Step 3: Price Positioning Analysis

"Based on the comp analysis, recommend a pricing strategy for [ADDRESS]. The seller wants $[THEIR EXPECTATION]. Comps range from $[LOW] to $[HIGH]. Provide:
- Recommended list price with justification
- Strategic pricing tiers (aggressive/moderate/conservative)
- Expected days on market for each tier
- Anticipated offer range
- Price adjustment strategy if needed

Use local [YOUR MARKET] market data and current trends."

Step 4: Create Price Justification Document

"Turn this pricing recommendation into a one-page document I can leave with the seller. Include:
- Visual price comparison chart
- Detailed justification for recommended price
- Market trend context
- Risk of overpricing
- Opportunity cost calculations

Make it visually clear and seller-friendly."

DIFFERENTIATION STRATEGY

Step 5: Marketing Plan Development

"Create a comprehensive marketing plan for [ADDRESS] that differentiates from the [NUMBER] competing listings currently active. My unique strategies include: [LIST YOUR SPECIAL MARKETING TACTICS]. Format as a presentation-ready document showing:
- Professional photography/video plan
- Digital marketing strategy (social, email, website)
- Traditional marketing (signs, flyers, open houses)
- Agent network outreach
- Timeline and milestones
- Competitive advantages"

THE PRESENTATION BUILDER

Step 6: Generate Your Listing Presentation Script

Master Prompt:

"Create a 15-minute listing presentation script for [ADDRESS]. Include:
- Opening (rapport building, agenda setting)
- Market analysis presentation (using the comp data we analyzed)
- Pricing strategy explanation
- Marketing plan overview
- My track record and testimonials section
- Objection handling (commission, contract length, dual agency)
- Closing (asking for the listing)

Tone: Confident, consultative, data-driven. Include transition phrases and suggested visual aids."

Comet Will Generate:

  • Word-for-word script with timing cues
  • Suggested slides/visual aids for each section
  • Transition language between topics
  • Questions to ask the seller
  • Closing techniques

USE CASE #5: OBJECTION RESPONSE LIBRARY

Saving Dying Deals

WHY THIS MATTERS

The difference between top producers and average agents is objection handling. Every "no" is an opportunity if you have the right response. Comet helps you prepare for and respond to objections in real-time.

BUILDING YOUR OBJECTION LIBRARY

Step 1: Identify Your Top 10 Objections

Common Real Estate Objections:

  • "The price is too high"
  • "I need to think about it"
  • "I want to see more properties first"
  • "We're going to keep renting"
  • "The inspection report scared us"
  • "We need to sell our current home first"
  • "Your commission is too high"
  • "We want to wait for rates to drop"
  • "The neighborhood isn't perfect"
  • "We found something we like better"

Step 2: Generate Response Framework

For Each Objection, Use This Prompt:

"Create a professional response to the objection: '[SPECIFIC OBJECTION]' in a real estate context. Include:
- Empathy statement (acknowledge their concern)
- Reframe (change perspective)
- Data/evidence (facts that address the concern)
- Alternative solution (if applicable)
- Trial close (move conversation forward)

Situation: [DESCRIBE SPECIFIC SCENARIO - buyer/seller, price point, property type, market conditions]

Tone: Understanding but confident, consultative not defensive."

EXAMPLE: "THE PRICE IS TOO HIGH" OBJECTION

Prompt:

"Create a response to a buyer saying 'The price is too high' for a $650K home in [YOUR MARKET]. Recent comps support the price. They love the property but feel it should be $600K. Include data, reframing, and path forward."

What You'll Get:

  • Empathy: "I understand this is a significant investment..."
  • Reframe: "Let's look at value vs. price..."
  • Data: "Here's what comparable homes actually sold for..."
  • Solution: "What if we structured an offer with..."
  • Close: "Would you be comfortable at $X with these terms?"

Then Customize:

• "Make this response more specific to luxury buyers"
• "Add a story about another client who had the same concern"
• "Include current market urgency factors"

REAL-TIME OBJECTION HANDLING

During Calls/Appointments:

Scenario: Client hits you with unexpected objection during showing

Quick Response Protocol:

Say: "That's a great point. Let me pull some data on that."
Open Comet sidebar (on phone or tablet)
Type: "Quick response to: [OBJECTION] - [CONTEXT]"
Read AI-generated talking points
Deliver confident, data-backed response
Practice This: Keep Comet open on your phone during showings. Get comfortable quickly asking for objection responses. It becomes your real-time coach.

SITUATION-SPECIFIC OBJECTION RESPONSES

Post-Inspection Objection Handling:

"Buyer received inspection report showing [LIST MAJOR ITEMS]. They want to back out or ask for $[AMOUNT] in repairs. Property is priced fairly at $[PRICE]. Create a negotiation strategy including:
- Which repairs are reasonable to request
- How to frame the response to seller
- Creative solutions (credit, price reduction, seller repairs)
- Comparable inspection reports from similar homes
- Path to keeping deal together"

Appraisal Gap Objection:

"Property appraised at $[APPRAISAL] but contract price is $[CONTRACT PRICE]. Buyer is frustrated, seller refuses to reduce. Create talking points for both parties including:
- Appraisal appeal strategy
- Alternative financing options
- Creative deal structures
- Market data supporting contract price
- Risk/reward analysis for each party"

Multiple Offer Objection (Buyer):

"My buyer is in a multiple offer situation for a property listed at $[PRICE]. They're frustrated and considering walking away. Create a strategy including:
- How to craft a competitive offer without overpaying
- Non-price terms that could win
- Escalation clause guidance
- Backup offer strategy
- When to walk away"

Cold Feet Before Closing:

"Buyer is getting cold feet 3 days before closing on a $[PRICE] home. They're worried about: [LIST CONCERNS]. Create a reassurance script including:
- Normal pre-closing anxiety validation
- Logic and data addressing each concern
- Long-term perspective
- Financial analysis confirming smart decision
- Action plan for moving forward confidently"

BONUS TIPS & BEST PRACTICES

PROMPT ENGINEERING FOR REAL ESTATE

The Formula for Great Prompts:

[ROLE] + [TASK] + [CONTEXT] + [FORMAT] + [TONE]

Example:

"As an experienced luxury real estate agent [ROLE], create a listing description [TASK] for a $2M waterfront property in Miami [CONTEXT] formatted as 3 paragraphs with bullet points [FORMAT] using elegant, aspirational language [TONE]."

Generic Prompt: "Write a listing description"

Better Prompt: "Write a compelling listing description for a 4bed/3bath single-family home in [NEIGHBORHOOD], priced at $[PRICE], emphasizing the renovated kitchen and large backyard, targeting young families, 150 words max."

SECURITY DISCLAIMER & BEST PRACTICES

IMPORTANT: READ BEFORE USING COMET WITH CLIENT DATA

CURRENT SECURITY CONCERNS

As of October 2025, independent security researchers have identified vulnerabilities in Perplexity's Comet browser that users should be aware of:

Identified Vulnerabilities:

  1. "CometJacking" Vulnerability: A critical zero-click vulnerability that could allow attackers to steal sensitive data including emails, calendar appointments, and credentials through malicious links.
  2. Indirect Prompt Injection: When asking Comet to summarize webpages, malicious content embedded in pages could potentially trick the AI into accessing information from other tabs or performing unintended actions.
  3. Data Access Concerns: Because Comet's AI can access authorized services (Gmail, Calendar, etc.), compromised AI instructions could theoretically access this data without additional authentication.

RECOMMENDED SECURITY PRACTICES

Until Perplexity Addresses These Issues:

DO:

  • Use Comet for generating content, research, and analysis of public information
  • Use it for drafting emails (then copy to your secure email client)
  • Leverage it for market research, listing descriptions, and marketing content
  • Practice the strategies in this workbook with non-sensitive data
  • Keep Comet updated to the latest version (security patches)

DO NOT:

  • Store sensitive client financial information in Comet's history
  • Input social security numbers, loan details, or confidential documents
  • Authorize Comet to access your Gmail if it contains sensitive client communications
  • Use it to access or store closing documents, purchase agreements, or legal contracts
  • Click unknown links while using Comet's AI features

BEST PRACTICES FOR REAL ESTATE PROFESSIONALS

1. Separate Browsers for Different Tasks:

  • Use Comet for: Content creation, market research, lead generation
  • Use secure browser (Chrome/Safari/Edge) for: CRM with client data, MLS backend, financial documents, e-signatures

2. Data Minimization:

  • Only share information with Comet that you'd be comfortable posting publicly
  • Assume anything entered into Comet could potentially be accessed
  • Remove client identifying information before analysis (use "Client A" instead of names)

3. Regular Security Checks:

  • Monitor Perplexity's security updates: www.perplexity.ai/security
  • Review browser permissions regularly
  • Log out of sensitive accounts when using Comet AI features

4. Client Consent:

  • Do not input client personal information into AI tools without disclosure
  • Follow NAR guidelines on data privacy and technology use
  • When in doubt, err on the side of client privacy

ALTERNATIVE SECURE WORKFLOWS

  1. Generate templates/frameworks in Comet (without client details)
  2. Export to your secure environment
  3. Add client-specific information in your secure CRM/email
